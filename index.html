Baked cabbage rolls

Baked cabbage rolls are prepared in south-east of Poland exclusively and are very unique and representative of the subcarpathian region.

Bigos

Bigos is a pickled cabbage and meat based dish. It is often a best way to utilise leftover hams, sausages and bacon. It is quite laborious in preparation, however the effort is definelly rewarded (...)

Boiled cabbage rolls

Boiled cabbage rolls are most commonly stuffed with minced pork and rice, however that can also be stuffed with other kinds of mince meat as well as different types of rice or buckwheat.

Chicken Soup

Chicken soup is the most popular soup across Poland. This soup is often a Sunday must have for many Poles. Easy to prepare, chicken soup is the favourite soup for many.

Dried fruit compote

Dried fruit compote is one of twelve traditional drinks and dishes consumed on Christmas Eve. However it is also a very refreshing drink in other seasons. Give this one a go at home!

Faworki

Faworki is an excellent snack for someone with a sweet tooth. This lightly sugar snack is well know and well liked throughout all parts of Poland.

Fish in jelly

Fish are important part of diet and should be eaten frequently therefore there is many fish-based dishes in Polish cuisine. Fish in jelly is another one of them and often seen on Polish Christmas Eve table.

Greek fish

Don’t be confused by the name of this dish. Although Greek fish originates from Greece it has become a traditional Christmas Eve dish as Poles do not eat any meat on Christmas Eve.

Mushroom and cabbage dumplings

Poland has many different types of dumplings to offer, however mushroom and cabbage dumplings are in general well liked throughout all parts of Poland.

Rus dumplings with spinach

These dumplings are a modern twist on the traditional rus dumplings. The addition of garlic and spinach transforms the simple and modest dumpling into a party on your mouth.

Rus dumplings

Rus dumplings are dumplings stuffed with potato and cottage cheese. They are not well known through Poland, but are rightfully appreciated in the south-east of Poland, mainly the Subcarpathian region.

Schnitzel

Although many may associate schnitzel with Germany, it is also one of the most common dinner dishes in Poland.

Smoked meat

Vegetable salad

Vegetable salad is one of the most common and traditional salads in Poland. Root vegetables such as potato, carrots and parsnip form the basis for this salad.
