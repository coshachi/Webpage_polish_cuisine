Smoked meat

Smoking meat is becoming a forgotten art in the land of Poland. Although nothing beats its taste, smoking meat is quite long process. Smoking meat requires some special equipment, however it is not hard in itself. Now days, with a vast variability and myriad of meats and ham to choose from in the marker.

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
EASY 0 h 25 min 0 h 25 min 6
Ingredients:
21 kg meat (ham or/and bacon)
9L water
10g pepper
4g marjoram
10 bay leaves
12g all spice
1 tsp coriander
1 tsp cumin
1 tsp sugar
5 garlic cloves (must be fresh)
700g curing salt

Method:

  1. Mix all herbs and spices in the 9L of water in a food-ready, clean barrel. Place portioned meat (wrapped with ham net, or tied with twine) in the barrel to cure for 12-16 days. Turn the meat upside-down every day.

  2. Take the meats out of the barrel and air dry and then dry with warm air in the smoke oven. Smoke the meat in a smoke over at 20-40°C for 12-14 hours. For best results use deciduous wood with bark removed.

  3. Smoke the meat in a smoke over at 20-40°C for 12-14 hours. For best results use deciduous wood with bark removed.

  4. Once smoking is completed place the meat in hot, but not boiling water (75-80°C) to brew for 1 hr per 1 kg of meat. Immediately after brewing place the meat into cold water to cool.
