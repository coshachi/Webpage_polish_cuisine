Faworki

Faworki is an excellent snack for someone with a sweet tooth. This lightly sugar snack is well know and well liked throughout all parts of Poland. It is generally prepared for a ‘Fat Thursday’ which is celebrated on the last Thursday before Ash Wednesday. Fat Thursday is dedicated to eating Faworki and doughnuts to mark the start of Lent.

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
EASY 0 h 25 min 0 h 25 min 6
Ingredients:
6 egg yolks
400g plain flour
12 tbsp sour cream
2 tsp baking powder
2 tbsp alcohol (vodka)
pinch of salt
oil for frying
icing sugar for decorating

Method:

  1. Mix together flour, egg yolks, baking powder, alcohol, salt and sour cream.
  2. Knead the dough, from time to time hit the dough with a roller until you can see air bubbles.
  3. Roll the dough into a thin sheet and cut into slices with 15cm in length and 3-4cm in width.
  4. Cut a small hole in each of the slices and put the tip of the slice through the hole so that little knot forms (faworki).
  5. Deep fry the faworki.
  6. Take out onto a sheet of kitchen towel to remove excess oil.

  7. Place the fried faworki on the plate and dust with icing sugar.
