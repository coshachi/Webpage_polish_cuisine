Schnitzel

Although many may associate schnitzel with Germany, it is also one of the most common dinner dishes in Poland. Living in apartment complexes, one can always hear the bang of the meat mallet at midday on Sundaysâ€™ and it only means one thing. Someone, somewhere is preparing the most traditional Polish Sunday dinner. Irish have their Sunday roast, while Poles have their Sunday schnitzel.

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
EASY 0 h 25 min 0 h 25 min 6
Ingredients:
2 boneless pork chops
2 eggs
50ml milk
bread crumbs
flour
1tsp mixed herbs
1tsp mixed herbs
oil

Method:

  1. Flatter the pork chops with meat mallet.

  2. In a flat wide bowl, whisk eggs, herbs, salt and pepper with fork, then add milk and whisk again. Spread some flour and bread crumbs on two separate plates. Cover the pork chop with flour, eggs and bread crumbs.

  3. Fry on a pan in hot oil.

  4. Serve with mash potatoes and beetroot mash or coleslaw.
