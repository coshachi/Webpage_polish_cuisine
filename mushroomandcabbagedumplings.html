Mushroom and cabbage dumplings

Poland has many different types of dumplings to offer, however mushroom and cabbage dumplings are in general well liked throughout all parts of Poland. They are often prepared for the traditional Christmas Eve dinner. Nevertheless, Poles do not need that excuse to keep preparing and eating them all year round.

Bon Appetit!

Method:

  1. DOUGH:
    Place the flour in a wide bowl. Add salt, oil and water. Mix ingredients together (can do it with the wooden spoon to begin with as the it might be hot). Mix the dough until it is soft and does not stick to your fingers. Wrap the dough in the kitchen foil and set aside for 30 min.
  2. STUFFING:
    Rinse cabbage in cold water 2-3 times to get rid of some of the acidity and chop. Boil the chopped cabbage with bay leaves, all spice and pepper. Pre-soak dry wild mushrooms overnight. Boil the mushrooms with a pinch of salt and chop. Fry the onion and when golden add chopped mushrooms and fry for another 10-15 min. Mix boiled cabbage and fried mushrooms together and allow to cool.
  3. COMBINED TOGETHER:
    Get about a third of the of the dough and roll into a thin sheet. Cut circles of the sheet dough with a glass. Place the stuffing in the middle of the circle, fold in half and stick the edges together with your fingertips. Repeat with the remaining dough. Once dumplings are ready, boil water in a big pot (salt water to taste). Place dumplings in the boiling water and cook until they float to the top and the water starts boiling again. Serve on a plate and sprinkle with some fried onion (optional).
