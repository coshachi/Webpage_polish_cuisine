Baked cabbage roll

In contrast to boiled cabbage rolls, baked cabbage rolls are, as the name suggests, prepared in the oven. Baked cabbage rolls are also prepared in south-east of Poland exclusively and are very unique and representative of the subcarpathian region. The baking process gives these cabbage rolls unique flavour. Try making the cabbage rolls both ways and pick your favourite!

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
HARD 1 h 30 min 3 h 30 min 4
Ingredients: Sauce Ingredients:
large savoy cabbage 500g or mushrooms
500g of minced pork or beef 2 medium onions
300g rice 200g sour cream
2 medium onions chicken stock cube
salt, pepper 1tbsp flour (for thickening the sauce)

Method:

  1. LEAVES:
    Remove outer green leaves from the cabbage (they are bitter). Cut out the stump of the cabbage. While brewing in boiling water, remove the leaves one by one using a fork and let them simmer until soft. Cut off the protruding thickened part from the main vein of each leaf.
  2. STUFFING:
    Dice and glaze the onion and add meat, salt and pepper. Fry until meat is done and remove excess liquid from the pan. In the meantime boil the rice. Mix fried meat and rice.
  3. SAUCE:
    Fry mushrooms with diced onion until soft. Add the sour cream, chicken stock cube, salt and pepper and bring to boil. Add 3 glasses of water and bring to boil again. In the meantime mix flour with half a glass of water, make sure there is no lumps and the mixture is smooth. Add a couple of spoons of your boiling sauce into the flour mixture (to warm it up) and mix. Pour the flour mixture into you sauce while constantly mixing. The sauce should thicken now. Bring to boil one more time and cook for 5 min on low heat.
  4. COMBINED TOGETHER:
    Spread out the cabbage leaf on the palm of your hand and add 2-3 tbsp of stuffing. Wrap up the stuffed leaf to form a roll. Place cabbage rolls in the heat resistant dish and cover. Bake at 180oC for 2 hours.

    Serve on the plate with a bit of sauce and enjoy.
