Bigos

Bigos is a pickled cabbage and meat based dish. It is often a best way to utilise leftover hams, sausages and bacon. It is quite laborious in preparation, however the effort is definelly rewarded by the test of this dish. Bigos tastes great from the moment it's done, nevertheless it develops its taste with time when all ingredients get a chance to infuse together and its taste peaks on day 2 or 3 after preparation.

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
MEDIUM 0 h 20 min 4 h 20 min 5
Ingredients:
2.5kg pickled cabbage
500g smoked bacon
500g smoked ham
500g Polish sausage
100g prunes
4 onions
50g dried wild mushrooms
50g raisins
5 tomatoes
tomato puree
4 bay leaves
6 grains of all spice
1 tbsp marjoram
1 tbsp paprika
1 tbsp oregano
salt and pepper to taste
1 glass red wine

Method:

  1. Rinse and chop the cabbage. Place in a big pot, cover with water and boil.

  2. Dice and fry onion until soft, then add diced sausage, ham and bacon and fry for further 15min. Add fried onion and meats to the cabbage.

  3. Peel tomatoes and add to the pot as well.

  4. Add bay leaves, all spice, marjoram, paprika, oregano, prunes and raisins to the pot and boil all together on low heat.

  5. Add red wine, tomato puree, salt and pepper.

  6. Cook the bigos for about 4 hr on very low heat mixing from time to time so that it doesnâ€™t stick to the bottom of the pot.

  7. Serve with bread or mashed potatoes. Enjoy!
