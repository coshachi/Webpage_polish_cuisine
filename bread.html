Bread

Bread forms the basis of Polish diet. Poles eat it for breakfast, supper and also as a snack. There’s many ways and methods of making the bread ranging from the simple one described here, to most complicated based on the sourdough that has to be constantly used and left behind as not to go off. And although recently carb-free lifestyle is becoming more and more popular, Poles will never be able to live without good homemade bread.

Bon Appetit!

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
MEDIUM 0 h 30 min 6 h 00 min 6
Ingredients:
500ml of lukewarm water
7g of active dry yeast
0.5kg all-purpose flour
1 ½ tsp salt
1 tsp olive oil

Method:

  1. Stir the yeast into the water and allow to bloom until foamy.

  2. In a large bowl, mix together the flour and salt. Create small well in the middle and pour in the water and yeast mixture. Mix by hand until the dough doesn’t stick to your fingers. If the dough is too sticky add small bit of flour. If it’s too dry add some water.

  3. Once dough comes together, cover and let rise for 2 hours.

  4. Work dough loose from the sides and fold it towards the centre. Cover and let the dough rise for another 2 hours.

  5. Gently transfer the dough into lightly floured surface and shape it into a loaf. Fold the dough under itself several times. Place the dough seam-side down into a clean bowl coated with olive oil. Cover and let rise for 1 hour.

  6. Meanwhile, place heavy cooking pot with oven safe lid in the oven and preheat to 230C. Remove the pot from the oven and place the dough inside (seam-side up). Cover and bake for 30 minutes then remove the lid and bake for further 10-15 minutes.

  7. Remove the bread from the pot and let cool. Enjoy!
