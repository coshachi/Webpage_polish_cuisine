Fish in jelly

Fish are important part of diet and should be eaten frequently therefore there is many fish-based dishes in Polish cuisine. Fish in jelly is another one of them and often seen on Polish Christmas Eve table. Although a traditional Christmas Eve dish, Fish in jelly is an excellent appetiser and can be served at friend or family gatherings.

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
MEDIUM 0 h 40 min 6 h 00 min 6
Ingredients:
medium-sized fish - about 1.5 kg (cod, salmon, trout or carp - according to your taste)
vegetables for broth: carrots, celery, leeks, parsley (1 of each)
3 hard-boiled eggs
1 egg white
2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
1 can of peas
fresh dill - bunch
gelatin -3 tsp
salt, cayenne pepper

Method:

  1. Use the vegetables to prepare vegetable broth.

  2. Cut fish into slices and cook in the vegetable both for 15-20 min. Do not boil!

  3. Gently remove the cooked fish and filter the broth through gauze. If the broth is not clear, clarify it with egg white (pout egg white and apple cider vinegar into hot but not boiling broth and whisk for 2-3 min, foam should form, then strain the broth through the gauze again).

  4. Add a pinch of cayenne pepper and salt to the clarified broth.

  5. In 1/2 cup of broth, dissolve 3 teaspoons of gelatin and add to the entire broth.

  6. Arrange the cooked fish on a dish in any way, garnish with a boiled egg, carrots, peas, sprinkle with chopped dill.

  7. Pour the broth with gelatin over the fish. Set aside to cool and put in the fridge for 5-6 hours.

  8. Serve with lemon wedges.
