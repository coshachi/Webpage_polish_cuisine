Greek fish

Don’t be confused by the name of this dish. Although Greek fish originates from Greece it has become a traditional (but not exclusive) Christmas Eve dish as Poles do not eat any meat on Christmas Eve. Aside from Christmas, Greek fish is often prepared for family and friends gatherings.

Difficulty Preparation time: Total time: Portions:
EASY 0 h 15 min 0 h 25 min 5
Ingredients: Sauce ingredients:
about 1.5 kg of frozen fish slices (pollock, hake, cod or other according to your taste) 4 medium carrots
oil 4 medium onions
flour tomato paste – 100g
2 eggs spicy ketchup - about 1/2 cup
salt

Fish:

  1. Defrost, dry fish with a paper towel and salt to taste.

  2. Cut into small pieces.

  3. Whisk eggs with fork, add salt, pepper and a bit of milk and whisk together.

  4. Coat fish pieces in flour, whisked eggs and flour again.

  5. Oil fry the fish pieces.

Sauce:

  1. Cut onion into quarters and slice.

  2. Grate carrots (coarse mesh).

  3. Fry onions and carrots together until semi-soft (al-dente).

  4. Add salt, pepper, tomato paste, ketchup and mix.

Combined:

  1. Layer the fried fish in a bowl placing hot sauce in between each layer.

  2. Cool down and serve.
